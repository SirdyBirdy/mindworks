About

A bit about Dimple

Dimple founded Mindworks in 2016 with one question: what would therapy look like if it actually respected the person in the room? Over twelve years, she's worked with over a thousand people on everything from clinical depression to the quieter kind of stuck — the kind where life looks fine on paper but feels hollow underneath.

Her approach draws on psychodynamic thinking, CBT, and a lot of honest conversation. She won't tell you what to do. She'll sit with you while you figure it out.

Specialties

What she works with

  • Clinical depression & mood disorders
  • Anxiety disorders (GAD, panic, social anxiety)
  • Relationship dynamics & attachment
  • Career transitions & performance pressure
  • Trauma & complex PTSD
  • Life transitions — marriage, divorce, parenthood
Approach

How she works

Sessions with Dimple tend to move between the present and the past — understanding the patterns that show up now by looking at where they started. She uses CBT tools when something practical is needed, and psychodynamic exploration when the question is bigger than a technique can answer.

She works best with people who are willing to be honest, even when it's uncomfortable. That's where the useful work usually lives.

FAQ

Common questions

Do you only work with clinical conditions?
No. About half the people I see don't have a diagnosis. They're just going through something hard and want someone to help them think through it.
What's the first session like?
We spend most of it understanding what's brought you in. I'll ask a lot of questions — not to assess you, but to get a real picture. By the end, we'll have a rough sense of how to work together.
What's the discovery call for?
The 15-minute discovery call is specifically for therapy enquiries only. It's a brief, no-pressure conversation to see if we're a good fit before you commit to a full session.
