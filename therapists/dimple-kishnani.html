Approach

How she works

"My hope is that clients don't have to hide parts of themselves, or perform in therapy. We make sense of things together, with curiosity rather than judgment."

Dimple works with Emotionally Focused Therapy (EFT), Internal Family Systems (IFS), trauma-informed therapy, DBT, and CBT, choosing between them depending on what's actually in front of her rather than sticking to one model. The aim isn't just to understand a pattern, it's to help it start to feel manageable.

Modalities she uses: EFT · IFS · Trauma-informed therapy · DBT · CBT

In the room

What does a session with Dimple actually feel like?

Warm and unhurried, without pressure to arrive at insight on cue. Dimple's approach is built around curiosity rather than judgment, so difficult emotions and patterns get room to become clearer instead of being rushed toward a conclusion.

Good fit

Who does she work especially well with?

People living with intense emotions or personality-related difficulties
Individuals healing from trauma or complex childhood experiences
Those who've experienced emotional neglect or invalidation
People struggling with their sense of identity or self-worth
Anyone looking to build a healthier relationship with themselves and others

Who might not be the best fit: Dimple works best with people who are open to self-reflection and willing to engage in the process. If you're looking for quick advice or someone to tell you exactly what to do, her approach may not be the right match.

Her story

How did she get into this work?

Dimple has always been curious about why people think, feel, and behave the way they do. That curiosity gradually grew into a deeper interest in emotional experience, and over time, psychology stopped being just an interest and became the work she wanted to dedicate herself to.

She founded Mindworks in 2016 because she kept seeing what therapy could look like when done with care, and how rarely that was the default. She wanted a practice where the quality of the relationship mattered as much as the clinical framework.

Outside of sessions, she's still thinking about human behaviour and emotional patterns. She reads psychology books alongside crime fiction and psychological thrillers, and fitness is a steady part of her life, a reminder of how physical and emotional wellbeing are connected.

Before you start

What she wishes people knew before starting therapy

"You don't have to be a different version of yourself to begin therapy. Every part of you is welcome here, even the parts you struggle to accept yourself."

Therapy isn't about being judged or fixed. It's a collaborative process, working through your experiences and building on your strengths at a pace that feels right for you.

FAQ

Common questions

Do you only work with people who have a diagnosis?
No. Plenty of people I work with don't have a diagnosis and don't need one. They're dealing with something hard and want support thinking it through. That's enough.
What's the first session actually like?
We spend most of it understanding what's brought you in. I'll ask a lot of questions, not to assess you clinically, but to get a real picture of you and your life. By the end, we'll have a rough sense of how we might work together.
How long does therapy usually take?
It depends on what you're working through. Some people find real relief in a handful of months. Others find more value in longer-term work. I'll be honest with you about what I think is needed, and you're always free to stop or pause.
What's the discovery call for?
It's a free 15-minute conversation, only for therapy enquiries. We use it to see if we're a good fit before you commit to a full session. No paperwork, no pressure.
Do you offer in-person sessions?
Yes, I see clients in person at our Pune clinic, as well as online. If you'd like to meet in person, mention that when you book and we'll arrange it.
