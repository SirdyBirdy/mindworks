About

A bit about Alina

Alina works with young adults who are figuring out identity, relationships, and how to exist in a world that didn't quite account for them. She brings a careful, curious approach — and no judgment.

She is queer-affirmative and trauma-informed, which means she understands that the context you came from shapes the way you feel now. Her work is collaborative and warm, grounded in CBT frameworks but flexible enough to follow where you need to go.

Approach

How she works

Alina uses CBT as a foundation, with trauma-informed practices woven in. She pays close attention to what gets left unsaid — the shame, the fear, the things you've never told anyone. Those are usually the things worth talking about.

Sessions are collaborative. You bring the direction; she helps you walk it, at whatever pace actually works.

FAQ

Common questions

I'm not sure I'm "queer enough" to see a queer-affirmative therapist.
Queer-affirmative just means I don't assume heterosexuality or cisgender identity as a default, and I won't treat your identity as something to be fixed. Anyone questioning, exploring, or existing outside the default is welcome here.
Do I have to talk about my trauma?
No. We go at your pace, always. Trauma therapy doesn't mean reliving everything — it means understanding how the past shows up in the present, and doing that carefully and only when you're ready.
What's the discovery call for?
It's a free 15-minute conversation — specifically for therapy enquiries only. We use it to see if we're a good fit before you commit to a full session. No paperwork, no pressure.
