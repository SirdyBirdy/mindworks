About

A bit about Armeet

Armeet works across anxiety, identity, and life transitions — with particular focus on helping people who feel caught between who they are and who they're supposed to be.

He brings a practical, structured approach — drawing on CBT, ACT, and REBT — while staying genuinely curious about the person in front of him. Sessions are focused, but never mechanical.

Approach

How he works

Armeet tends to work in a structured way — CBT for behavioural patterns, ACT for the harder-to-shift stuff, REBT for the beliefs underneath. But the structure is always in service of the person, not the other way round.

He adapts. If one framework isn't landing, he'll move. The goal is what actually helps you, not what fits neatly into a model.

FAQ

Common questions

I have OCD but I've heard therapy can make it worse. Is that true?
ERP (Exposure Response Prevention) is very effective for OCD when done carefully. I use a structured, evidence-based approach that is gradual and collaborative. Nothing happens without your consent and readiness.
Do you offer sessions in Punjabi?
Yes. If it's easier for you to express certain things in Punjabi, that's completely fine. We can mix languages within a session if that works better for you.
What's the difference between CBT, ACT, and REBT?
All three work with thoughts and behaviour, but in different ways. CBT challenges unhelpful thinking patterns. ACT focuses on accepting difficult feelings and committing to values-based action. REBT looks at the core beliefs that drive distress. I use all three — whichever fits what you're dealing with.
