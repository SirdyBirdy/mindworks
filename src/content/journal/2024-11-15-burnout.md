Most people don't recognise burnout while they're in it.

The common picture is someone collapsed, unable to get out of bed. But that's usually stage three. Stage one looks a lot like productivity — staying late because you're almost done, skipping lunch because you're not really hungry.

## What it actually feels like early on

Before the crash, burnout tends to feel like mild annoyance. The work you used to care about feels flat. Small things that wouldn't have bothered you six months ago now require visible effort to tolerate.

You might sleep eight hours and wake up tired. Not physically tired — a kind of heaviness that sits just behind your eyes.

## What helps

The most useful thing isn't a holiday, though rest matters. It's usually understanding what specifically is draining you — which often turns out to be something much more specific than the job.

Therapy doesn't fix burnout. But it can help you understand it — and that tends to make the difference.
